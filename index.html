What BCClarity gives you

Four capabilities built around the reality of what patients actually need — not what the clinical system assumes they need.

Treatment information

Clear, plain-language information about treatment options and protocols — especially for the months and years after initial diagnosis. We point to trusted sources and tell you what questions to ask.

Share your experience

Contribute your own treatment decisions, side effects, and outcomes — anonymously. See what others in similar circumstances have experienced. What you actually did matters as much as what you were prescribed.

Current research

Access to current publications, clinical findings, and emerging research — curated and translated into language that helps you advocate for yourself in the doctor's office.

Your personal log

Track your circumstances, decisions, reactions, and side effects over time — privately and anonymously. Your record belongs to you, and it may matter more than you realize years from now.

The conversation that isn't happening

"Your doctor assumes you're following every instruction. Most patients aren't — and they're not telling anyone why."

BCClarity was founded on a simple but uncomfortable truth: the gap between what physicians prescribe and what patients actually do is enormous — and almost entirely invisible to the clinical system.

A patient who halves their tamoxifen dose because the joint pain is unbearable. A patient who stops an aromatase inhibitor after six months. A patient who adds testosterone or supplements alongside their standard protocol. These decisions happen every day, and they're rarely disclosed to a physician — not out of deception, but out of fear of judgment, of being seen as non-compliant, of losing the relationship.

That silence has a cost. It means the data that would most help future patients — what people actually do, and what happens — never gets collected. BCClarity is building the place where that data can finally exist.

We're building this alongside patients, not for them. Every design decision starts with one question: does this serve the person who's struggling with a side effect at 2am, trying to figure out if anyone else has been through this?

Why your contribution matters

Clinical trials capture what physicians prescribe. No one captures what patients actually decide, why, and what happens. That data doesn't exist anywhere — yet.

Every person who contributes their experience to BCClarity makes the platform more useful for the next person who's navigating the same decision at midnight, alone, trying to figure out if she's the only one.

Supporters are welcome here too. If you're walking alongside someone with breast cancer, your perspective — what helped, what didn't, what you wish you'd known — is part of this story.

Your data belongs to you. BCClarity holds a license to aggregate and analyze it — never to sell it. You can update or remove your contribution at any time.
